### 3. Candidate Profile Criteria

**Must-Have Skills (Technical & Soft):**
*   **Technical:**
    *   Strong proficiency in **Python** with significant experience in **Django and/or Flask**.
    *   Expertise in **RESTful API design and development** (building scalable, secure, and performant APIs).
    *   Solid understanding and hands-on experience with **database design** using **PostgreSQL** and/or **MongoDB**.
    *   Practical experience with **AWS services** (EC2, S3, Lambda, RDS, SQS, etc.).
    *   Demonstrable experience with **video streaming protocols** and **CDN integration** (understanding how video content is delivered efficiently).
    *   Experience implementing and working with **Microservices architecture**.
    *   Experience optimizing backend services for **high-traffic scenarios** (handling millions of concurrent users).
*   **Soft Skills:**
    *   **Ownership and Accountability:** Takes full responsibility for features/modules.
    *   **Problem-Solving:** Strong analytical and debugging skills.
    *   **Collaboration:** Experience working effectively with product and frontend teams in an Agile environment.
    *   **Mentorship:** Ability to guide and mentor junior engineers (as this is a senior role).
    *   **Adaptability:** Thrives in a fast-paced, high-growth startup environment.

**Preferred Experience (Years, Domains, Company Types):**
*   **Experience:** 4-6 years of professional software development experience, specifically in backend roles.
*   **Domains:** Direct experience in **OTT, media, entertainment, or streaming platforms** is highly preferred. Companies dealing with large-scale content delivery or high-volume user traffic.
*   **Company Types:** Fast-growing startups, product companies with a strong engineering culture, or divisions within larger companies focused on digital media/streaming.

**Red Flags to Avoid:**
*   **Lack of Hands-on Scale Experience:** Candidates who have only worked on small-to-medium scale projects and lack exposure to high-traffic, concurrent user scenarios.
*   **Purely Frontend/DevOps/Data Science:** While related, the core expertise must be backend development.
*   **Only Theoretical Knowledge:** Mentions technologies without demonstrating practical, in-depth usage, especially in the context of system design and problem-solving.
*   **Job Hopping:** Multiple very short stints (less than 1 year) without clear explanations (e.g., contract roles).
*   **No Mentorship/Leadership Inclination:** For a "Senior" role, an ability or willingness to mentor is crucial.
*   **Inexperience with Cloud/Microservices:** If they haven't worked with AWS or designed microservices, it's a significant gap.

**Nice-to-Have Qualifications:**
*   Direct experience with **video encoding/transcoding workflows**.
*   Knowledge of specific protocols like **HLS/DASH**.
*   Familiarity with **Redis** for caching/session management and **Celery** for asynchronous task processing.
*   Experience with **Docker and Kubernetes** for containerization and orchestration.
*   Contributions to open-source projects, especially in Python, Django, or streaming tech.

### 4. Sourcing Strategy

**Where to Find These Candidates (Platforms, Communities, Events):**

1.  **LinkedIn Recruiter:**
    *   Utilize advanced filters: "Senior" experience level, relevant skills (Python, Django, AWS, OTT), industry (Internet, Broadcast Media, Entertainment), company size, current/past companies.
    *   Look for candidates with specific certifications (AWS).
    *   Identify "People also viewed" on profiles of target candidates.
2.  **GitHub:**
    *   Search for Python/Django/Flask projects, especially those related to video processing, streaming, or large-scale APIs.
    *   Look at contributors to popular open-source projects in the media/streaming tech space.
    *   Review profiles for project contributions, coding style, and problem-solving approaches.
3.  **Stack Overflow:**
    *   Identify highly active users in Python, Django, AWS, Microservices, and streaming-related tags.
    *   Look at their reputation and contributions to gauge expertise.
4.  **AngelList / Product Hunt:**
    *   Identify similar OTT/media/streaming startups and check their team pages or look for engineers who previously worked there.
    *   Connect with founders/CTOs of relevant startups for referrals.
5.  **Tech Communities & Forums:**
    *   **Python India / Django India / Flask India** communities (Slack, Discord, Meetup groups).
    *   **AWS User Groups** (Noida/Delhi NCR chapters).
    *   Specific **streaming tech communities** or forums (e.g., Demuxed community, Video Developers Meetup).
6.  **Company Career Pages (Competitors):**
    *   Regularly check career pages of direct competitors (Hotstar, Zee5, Voot, JioCinema, SonyLIV, MX Player) and companies with similar tech challenges (e.g., large e-commerce, gaming platforms).
    *   Look for engineers whose profiles indicate they are ready for a new challenge or have recently left these companies.
7.  **Referral Programs:**
    *   Leverage internal employee networks for referrals. Offer attractive referral bonuses.
    *   Tap into your own professional network.
8.  **Online Coding Platforms:**
    *   Platforms like HackerRank, LeetCode, CodeChef often have public profiles or leaderboards where top engineers can be identified (though often less direct for senior roles).
